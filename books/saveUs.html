"No One Is Coming to Save Us"

Author: Stephanie Powell Watts

Genre: Contemporary Fiction, Literary Fiction
Publication Date: April 4, 2017

"No One Is Coming to Save Us" by Stephanie Powell Watts is a contemporary novel that reimagines the themes of F. Scott Fitzgerald’s "The Great Gatsby" in a modern-day African American community in Pinewood, North Carolina. The story centers around J.J. Ferguson, a young man who returns to his hometown after spending years away building a successful career in construction. J.J. arrives in Pinewood with grand plans: he’s built a luxurious mansion on the hill and intends to win back Ava, the love of his youth, whom he left behind. Ava, however, is now married to Henry, a factory worker whose once-promising career has been stalled by economic downturns. She is struggling with the stagnation of her marriage and the painful desire for a child that she cannot seem to have. J.J.’s return throws her life into turmoil as she is forced to confront the choices she made and the dreams she left unfulfilled. Meanwhile, Sylvia, Ava’s mother, is grappling with her own disillusionment. She is trying to hold her family together while dealing with the death of her son, Don, and her husband’s failing health. As J.J. attempts to recreate his version of the American Dream, building a house and a future that he hopes will entice Ava to start over with him, the novel delves into the lives of each character, revealing their hopes, regrets, and the harsh realities of living in a town where dreams often go unfulfilled. Through rich and nuanced storytelling, Watts explores themes of aspiration, loss, racial and economic divides, and the lengths people will go to for love and redemption. The novel also paints a vivid picture of the community of Pinewood, capturing the challenges faced by those in economically disadvantaged, rural towns across America. The decline of the manufacturing industry has left many residents struggling to find stable work, and the pervasive sense of loss and frustration is palpable. Against this backdrop, the characters are all searching for something more—something to make their lives meaningful and fulfilling. Stephanie Powell Watts skillfully weaves together the personal and the political, highlighting the complexities of race, class, and the American Dream. The narrative moves fluidly between the perspectives of J.J., Ava, Sylvia, and others, allowing readers to see the interconnectedness of their lives and the ways in which their pasts shape their present. Each character is deeply flawed yet profoundly human, making their struggles and triumphs both relatable and compelling. "No One Is Coming to Save Us" is a poignant exploration of the pursuit of happiness and the illusions we create to achieve it. Watts’ writing is lyrical and evocative, drawing readers into the world of Pinewood and the inner lives of its residents. The novel is a testament to the resilience of the human spirit and the enduring power of hope, even in the face of seemingly insurmountable odds.
